Overview

The International Institute of Saint Louis is a key resource for immigrants in Eastern Missouri. Among their other resources and efforts, they have an Anti-Trafficking In Persons Program team that focuses on identifying and aiding victims of human trafficking. As one of the only ATIP teams in St. Louis that works with labor trafficking victims, my work with the team mainly focused on the creation of accessible resources for clients in English and Spanish as well as the analysis and identification of trends in labor violation data.

  • Role: Research, data-analysis, and design
  • Tools: R Studio, Procreate, and Figma
  • Duration: 7 months

Exploration

Working with the ATIP team really allowed me to put into practice one of my key beliefs: Prescriptive solutions can be more harmful than good, which is why data-based solutions are essential in tackling social issues. During my time with the ATIP team I was able to identify, clean, and analyze data official data sets on labor practices. The goal of this analysis was to bring light to some of the current trends in labor law violations, immigrant workers, and the industries in which labor trafficking might be encountered and identify major labor law violators to look out for. Specifically I looked at the following resources:

  • US Department of Labor
  • US Citizenship and Immigration Services
  • National Center for Farmworker Health

Data Cleaning

After compiling the data, R Studio was used to process, clean, and analyze it in search of possible trends. The data was filtered by the following locations: Saint Louis, Chesterfield, Clayton, Kirkwood Florissant, Saint Charles, O'fallon, Wentzville, Lake Saint Louis, Arnold, Festus, Hillsboro, and De Soto. Due to the nature of the data, entries with NAs in key variables was excluded. This data goes up to 2024 and is not influenced by new labor and immigration laws of 2025.

Analysis Methods

As an initial exploratory approach, most of the descriptive analysis focuses on the relationships between two or more variables. Due to the nature of the data and the research objective, this is not meant to show or prove any causation, but rather explore possible correlations. It is divided into four sections: Wage and Hour Violations, OSHA Violations, Foreign Labor Data, and H Visa Employer Data.

Wage and Hour Violations

Following the Fair Labor Standards Act, the Department of Labor has established guidelines and norms to ensure workers rights to fair wages. They conduct investigations on reports of discriminatory and illegal practices in all legitimate labor industries. The data was initially cleaned and filtered to focus on the city and industry as main variables, with the number of investigations and violations being examined for each. This is meant to highlight patterns in the wage and hour violations reported in different industries, pointing at particular spots that we want to keep an eye on. The analysis uses registered data from 2018 to 2024.
