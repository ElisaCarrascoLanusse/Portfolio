Overview

During my time as a Sociology student at Grinnell College I got to collaborate with faculty and peers on meaningful research. One such project is "Memory Matters: A Comparative Analysis of the Commemoration, Memorialization, and Reconciliation Processes of Genocide in Rwanda, Bosnia, and Cambodia". Working with Meghna Adhikari, Ileah Bonzi, and Dr. Sharon Quinsaat we analyzed genocide memorial sites in order to answer the question: How do different actors influence the framing of memorialization sites?

  • Role: Research and writing
  • Tools: Zotero, Canva
  • Duration: 4 months

Exploration

The bulk of my research was on the case of the 1994 Rwandan (Tutsi) genocide (800,000 to 1 million people killed), specifically the Kigali Genocide Memorial. This specific site was chosen due to its location in the capital and role in the post-genocide reconciliation process and efforts. Starting with the historical and socio-political context, I was able to understand the ways in which colonial legacies directly impacted the Hutu majority's genocide of the Tutsi people. This was particularly helpful in understanding the conditions leading up to the genocide and the international response, or lack thereof in the face of such atrocities.

After understanding the context, I analyzed the memorialization process. The Kigali Genocide Memorial was established in 2004 as a designated place for the mass graves of 250,000 residents murdered during the genocide. The site includes memorial gardens, a wall of names, graves, and the Genocide Archive of Rwanda. In actuality, it is a center for Rwandans and tourists alike to learn about the genocide, reconciliation, and peacebuilding.

Research Presentation

Research

The research process begun by defining the target audience. The Partnerships team works directly with donors and partners, so based on this I created three user personas:

  • Corporate Donor
  • Individual Donor
  • Partner Donor

After defining the target audience, I conducted a survey to understand the current perception of the newsletter. The survey was sent to 100 current donors and partners, and the results were as follows:

  • 60% of respondents found the newsletter "informative"
  • 30% of respondents found the newsletter "engaging"
  • 10% of respondents found the newsletter "confusing"

Solution

Based on the research, I worked on a new design for the newsletter. The design focused on improving the following:

  • Visual hierarchy
  • Content organization
  • Call to action

Conclusion

After implementing the new design, the newsletter saw an increase in engagement:

  • The average open rate increased to 35.10%
  • The average click rate increased to 7.50%
  • The average unsubscribing rate decreased to 1.04%
